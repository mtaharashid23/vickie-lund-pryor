About The Book

PICKS, SHOVELS, AND RIFLES

The Journal of an Army Engineer in WWI is a compelling firsthand account of World War I through the eyes of Sgt. Philip Frank “P.F.” Lund of Company A, 6th U.S. Engineers A.E.F. Drawn from his original journals and photographs, it captures the daily challenges, danger, humor, and determination of an ordinary American serving in extraordinary times.

From enlisting in Connecticut at age 28 to building bridges, fortifications, and vital infrastructure near the front lines, Lund reveals the crucial but often overlooked role of U.S. Army Engineers—those who built victory one plank, one trench, one mile at a time.

Amidst mud, cold, and constant uncertainty, he reflects on faith, loyalty, and brotherhood while painting vivid scenes of life in wartime France. Readers join him in long marches, brief moments of laughter, and the comfort of mail from home.

Carefully transcribed and edited by his granddaughter, Vickie Lund Pryor, this book honors Lund’s service and the quiet heroism of all engineers who served in World War I—a testament to duty, endurance, and a legacy carried forward through generations.
